Growth Rate

To find the growth rate of a population, enter two population figures. The chronologically first value should be placed in the initial population field, while the later population figure is placed in the Current Population field. Enter the amount of years between the two statistics in the "years passed" field.

Example:
Canada had a population of 34.75 million in 2012, and a population of 35.16 million in 2013. Enter 34.75 in the Initial Population, 35.16 in Current Population, and 1 in Years Passed. The result given is 0.0118. Rounded this is 0.012, or 1.2% annual change, the official population growth statistic if you search on Google.
